# Component: PETG_Sleeves

## Description

Tiny printed bearing sleeves that form the concealed piano-wire hinge system. Each sleeve is a short tube (1.2mm OD, 0.6mm ID, 3mm long) printed in PETG for its low friction and wear resistance. A total of 48 sleeves are used (24 per half-span), threaded onto the 0.5mm music wire in alternating fashion: odd sleeves are bonded to the stab shell, even sleeves are bonded to the elevator shell, creating a distributed hinge with zero external hardware.

## Assembly Notes

- Thread all 48 sleeves onto the 0.5mm music wire before final assembly. Alternate between stab-fixed and elevator-fixed sleeves along the span.
- Bond stab-fixed sleeves into the hinge saddle recesses with a micro drop of thin CA. Bond elevator-fixed sleeves into the bull-nose recesses similarly.
- Ensure each sleeve rotates freely on the wire before bonding its neighbor. If a sleeve binds, ream with a 0.6mm drill bit.
- PETG was chosen over PLA for its lower friction coefficient against steel and better fatigue resistance at the hinge bearing interface.
- These are extremely small parts. Print a few spares (recommend 56 total) in case of loss or damage during assembly.
